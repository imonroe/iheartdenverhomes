Every Denver neighborhood has its own personality, pace, and price point. Here's my local take on the areas I know and love best.

Cherry Creek

Upscale living,
walkable luxury

Cherry Creek is Denver's premier shopping and dining district. Tree-lined streets, independent boutiques, world-class restaurants, and some of the city's most sought-after real estate — all without leaving the neighborhood. It's urban walkability at its most polished.

Washington Park

Denver's most
beloved park

Wash Park (as locals call it) revolves around its iconic 165-acre park with two lakes, miles of jogging paths, and summer volleyball leagues. The surrounding neighborhood is full of craftsman bungalows, charming streets, and a tight-knit community feel that's hard to find anywhere else in the city.

LoHi · Lower Highlands

Denver's trendiest
dining destination

LoHi is where Denver's best restaurants, rooftop bars, and coffee shops have converged. With stunning views of the downtown skyline and easy access to the Platte River trail, it attracts young professionals and design-forward buyers who want to be in the center of the action.

Highlands

Historic charm,
vibrant community

One of Denver's most desirable neighborhoods, the Highlands has deep historic roots and a thriving commercial strip along 32nd Avenue. Victorian homes, local coffee shops, independent restaurants, and a welcoming community make this a perennial favorite for buyers of all ages.

Congress Park

Quiet streets,
eclectic soul

Congress Park sits between Cherry Creek and City Park, giving residents access to both. It's a quieter, more residential neighborhood with beautiful older homes, tree-shaded blocks, and a loyal local community. Ideal for buyers who want character and calm without sacrificing convenience.

RiNo · River North

Art, craft beer,
and creative energy

RiNo (River North Art District) is Denver's creative hub — murals cover warehouse walls, craft breweries anchor every block, and innovative restaurants pop up constantly. It's the neighborhood for buyers who want something genuinely different: lofts, new construction, and an electric energy that's all its own.

Wheat Ridge

Ranch homes,
real community

Wheat Ridge is Emily's home — and she'll tell you it's one of Denver's best-kept secrets. With mid-century ranch homes on generous lots, a thriving local restaurant scene, and easy mountain access, it offers incredible value just minutes from Denver. It's the neighborhood where people put down real roots.
